Gallaudet

Gallaudet University, named after Thomas Gallaudet, is the worldâ€™s only Deaf liberal arts college. All or almost all classes there are taught in ASL. It has been seen as a Deaf mecca, as it is a place that Deaf people have gathered from all around the world.

The "Deaf President Now" protest was a very important event in Deaf history. This protest lasted 7 days. It was a protest done by the students at Gallaudet because the university had hired a hearing president over 2 other Deaf candidates. The students marched and shut the campus down for a week. The protest ended in the appointment of I. King Jordan, a Deaf president.
The students demands were as follows:
1. Zinser (the hearing president) resign and a Deaf president be selected.
2. The immediate resignation of Jane Bassett Spilman, chair of Board of Trustees.
3. The reconstituion of the Board of Trustees with 51% majority of Deaf members.
4. There be no reprisals against any students or staff members involved in protest.
