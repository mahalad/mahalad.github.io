Deaf Culture

Deaf Culture: ASL is its own language. It has grammar and syntax like any other language. There are many aspects to Deaf culture. An important part of Deaf culture is that ASL is encouraged. Over the years, Deaf people have faced a lot of oppression, their language was deemed not real, they were forced to speak, and many other things.

Deaf people are very proud of their Deaf identity and want that to be respected in their community. The Deaf community does not have a geographical location tied to it. They are found spread across the U.S. Deaf people do not want deafness to be seen as a disability. Instead, they want to be seen as their own culture and own group. They simply want equality and rights.
